Welcome to MacSailing

Canoeing camps take kids off the beach and onto the water in a different way. You'll paddle them to beaches, run drills, and build confidence on the water.

MacSailing's canoeing program runs alongside sailing camps all summer. You'll work with youth groups from Beach Kids (ages 6–7) through older camp groups. The goal is the same across all ages — safe on the water, engaging on the beach, working as a team.

A Typical Day

The shape of every session — from setup to sign-out.

8:30

Arrive & Setup — Paid Time

First canoe instructor grabs class list from the office. Two instructors set up canoes and coach boats — check class list for numbers needed. Others set up classroom and whiteboard.

9:00

Welcome the Class

Welcome campers. Ice breakers and name games on Day 1. Classroom briefing — canoe parts, paddle technique, safety rules, what to expect on the water.

9:30

Get Changed & Life Jackets

Bring attendance to put back in the office. Help with wetsuits — try to remember sizes from Day 1. Check in with beach coach about launch timing.

~10:00

Launch

Coach boat must be on the water before the class launches. Split class into canoes with equal strengths. Ensure aft paddler can steer. Do not launch until coach in coach boat gives thumbs up.

On water

On-Water Session

Run drills with skill progression. Go against the current on the first half. Don't go too far out on windy days. Keep coach boat close — especially near any capsizes.

11:30

Return for Lunch & Sign-Out

Return by 11:30–11:40 latest. Return life jackets and wetsuits. Three instructors: one directs full-day students to lunch, one stays to greet afternoon students, one manages half-day sign-out.

12:00

Your 30-Minute Lunch

Switch off with other instructors. Even if you start after 12:30 you still take the full 30 minutes. Be ready for afternoon students at 11:50.

1:00

Afternoon Session

Repeat morning flow. Same energy for the afternoon group.

15:30

Return & Pack Down

Head back by 15:30–15:40. Have kids help put canoes away before changing. One instructor checks off names at pick-up. Others put away coach boats and clean up.

16:30

Sign Out

Do not leave until everything is put away — help other programs too. Let a supervisor know if you need to leave early.

⚠️
Do not leave until everything is put away. This means helping instructors from all programs, not just canoeing.

Coach Boats & Engine

Know your coach boat before you need it. Every item has a reason.

Coach Boat Contents — Check Every Session

🏏 At least one paddle

In case the engine fails — you need a way to move the boat.

🪢 Two towlines (coiled)

For towing disabled canoes. Must be coiled — tangled lines are useless in an emergency.

⚓ Bow line

For tying up to the dock.

🪣 Bailer

For removing water from the coach boat.

💨 Pump (some boats)

Check if your assigned boat needs one — not all do.

🚫 No garbage

Keep the coach boat clean at all times.

Engine Parts — Know These Cold

PartWhat it doesKey note
Steering tillerSteers the boatPush left = go right, push right = go left
Throttle (twist handle)Controls speedMust be in neutral before starting
Pull-start handleStarts the engineThrottle to neutral first — you'll feel resistance as gears change
Kill cord switchCuts the engineFlip switch UP to arm before starting
Kill cordEmergency stopTeardrop over kill switch. Clip to yourself — always.
Off buttonNormal shutdownUse this for normal engine shutdown
🔑
Kill cord rule — non-negotiable: Clip the kill cord to yourself before starting the engine. Remove it only after the engine is off. If you fall out, the engine stops automatically.

Starting the Engine

1

Throttle to neutral

You should feel resistance as gears change. Do not start until you feel this.

2

Secure the kill cord

Teardrop attachment over kill switch. Flip switch UP. Clip cord to yourself.

3

Pull-start

Pull the handle firmly. If it doesn't start, check throttle is in neutral and kill cord is properly attached.

Launching

Your responsibility starts before the first canoe hits the water.

Before Launching

  • All instructors on the same page before anyone is near the water
  • One coach in a coach boat waiting before the class launches
  • Radio on, high volume — before you leave the beach
  • Have everything you need before the water — the beach coach is very busy during launching. Do not rely on them.

Launching Sequence

  • Split the class into canoes with equal strengths
  • Ensure the aft paddler is capable of steering
  • Boats not in water until instructor or volunteer is monitoring from a coach boat
  • Boats in water and ready — do not launch until coach in coach boat gives thumbs up
  • If possible, have a coach boat out already so you can head straight to the course

Current & Navigation

  • Go against the current on the first half of the paddle
  • If pushed west, aim for an eastern beach and vice versa
  • Return will usually take longer — plan for it
  • Don't go too far out on windy days

Capsize Recovery

🌊
Turn your engine OFF before getting close to people in the water. Every time, no exceptions.

Pull kids in by their life jacket shoulder straps first. Get floating gear after people are safe. Have class move to back of canoes when being towed.

When Something Goes Wrong

1

Immediate danger? Radio and act now

Call for help immediately. Do not wait.

2

Secure the rest of the class

Another instructor supervises the group. Don't leave them unattended.

3

Stay with the problem

Do not leave until resolved. Calling for help is not failure — it's the right call.

4

Report everything

Incident report. Tell your supervisor. Notify the parent.

Safety

Know every scenario before you need it.

⚠️
Every incident — no matter how small — must be communicated to another staff member immediately. Big incidents go to a supervisor. Always file an incident report and notify the parent.

Emergency Response

SituationAction
Small scrape or cutRefer to MacSailing Office
Head hit — no concussion signsRefer to MacSailing Office
Severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis)Refer to MacSailing Office + Activate EAP
Major head injury / loss of consciousnessRefer to MacSailing Office + Activate EAP
Mild hypothermia or heat exhaustionRefer to MacSailing Office + Activate EAP
Heat stroke or severe hypothermiaRefer to MacSailing Office + Activate EAP
📋
Always: File an incident report using the Incident & Repairs Form. Parent must be notified — no exceptions.

Head & Spine Injuries

  • Ask: "Do you feel pain in your neck or back? Any tingling in your fingers or toes?"
  • Ask: "Can you squeeze my hands? Can you wiggle your toes?"
  • Pain in neck/back, tingling, or unequal grip/wiggle = TRUE EMERGENCY
  • Call 911. Hold head stable. Tell them not to move. Do not move them.
